Common Problems We Solve

Where Medical Practices Lose Time and Revenue

⚠
Inconsistent Patient Intake

Every front desk staff member runs intake differently. Information collected varies, patient experience varies, and no one can be held accountable because there is no documented standard to hold them to.

⚠
Prior Authorization Delays

Prior authorizations are submitted late, tracked informally, or missed entirely — causing delayed care, patient frustration, and claim denials that require expensive rework or write-offs.

⚠
Clinical-Admin Communication Gaps

Clinical and administrative staff operate in parallel rather than as an integrated team. Information gets lost in handoffs, verbal-only communication creates errors, and there is no documented escalation path.

⚠
Provider as Operational Bottleneck

The physician or provider makes operational decisions that should be delegated — because no one else has the documented authority or guidance to handle them. Every decision runs through a clinical leader who should be focused on patient care.

Frequently Asked Questions

Medical Practice Consulting FAQ

Do you work with specialty practices?

Yes. We work with primary care, specialty, and multi-specialty practices. The core operational challenges — intake, prior authorization, documentation, onboarding, communication — are consistent across specialties, and we adapt our frameworks to the specific workflows of your practice type.

We use Epic / Athena / Cerner. Can you still help?

Yes. Our work focuses on the processes around your EMR — intake workflows, documentation standards, prior auth tracking, staff procedures — not the EMR itself. We have direct experience with Epic, Cerner, and Meditech environments.

What is the difference between you and a practice management consultant?

We are an RN-led operations firm. That means the clinical insight embedded in our documentation, escalation protocols, and workflow design is grounded in direct nursing and healthcare experience — not a business-only framework applied to a clinical environment.

Can you help with HIPAA-compliant workflow documentation?

Yes. HIPAA awareness is embedded in how we design every workflow and documentation standard. We build systems that support compliance rather than create risk.

Result 01

Home Care Agency: Lost Referrals to Structured Growth

A home care agency founder came to us spending 10+ hours per week managing intake manually — and still losing referrals.

Before

  • Referrals tracked in a personal notebook — no system, no visibility
  • No standardized intake process or intake documentation forms
  • Follow-up happened when someone remembered to do it
  • Owner personally handling every intake call regardless of time
  • New caregivers required weeks of individual supervision
  • No written SOPs for any critical operational process
  • Missed follow-ups — no way to know how many referrals were lost
  • Owner was the single point of failure for every operational decision

After

  • Structured referral tracking with source data, status flags, and follow-up triggers
  • Complete intake SOP with standardized forms, scripts, and information requirements
  • 48-hour follow-up workflow with accountability checkpoints and documentation
  • Staff handling intake independently using written protocols — owner optional
  • Onboarding checklist reducing new caregiver supervision time significantly
  • Complete SOP library covering intake, scheduling, coordination, and escalation
  • Referral response time reduced from 3–5 days to under 48 hours
  • Owner reclaimed approximately 10 hours per week previously spent on manual intake

Key Takeaway

The problem was not a lack of effort. The problem was a lack of systems. Once the intake and follow-up workflows were documented and delegated, the agency had the operational foundation to scale without the owner as the operational bottleneck.

DELIVERABLES: Referral Tracking System ⋅ Intake SOP ⋅ Follow-Up Workflow ⋅ Staff Onboarding Checklist ⋅ Complete SOP Library

Result 02

Medical Spa: Scheduling Chaos to Streamlined Operations

A medical spa owner managing scheduling through personal texts, absorbing no-shows without a system, and building a client base without a way to retain them.

Before

  • Scheduling managed through personal texts and phone calls — no system
  • No-show rate averaging 20%+ with no follow-up protocol
  • No CRM — client history, preferences, and treatment records lived in staff memory
  • New client experience varied depending on who answered the phone
  • No automated reminders, confirmations, or follow-up sequences
  • Inconsistent intake — different staff collected different information
  • Post-treatment follow-up happened inconsistently or not at all

After

  • CRM implemented with complete client history, service tracking, and notes
  • Automated appointment reminder sequence — 48-hour and same-day confirmation
  • Standardized new client onboarding protocol with consistent information collection
  • Scheduling SOP giving staff a clear, documented process to follow
  • No-show follow-up and rescheduling workflow reducing lost appointment revenue
  • Post-treatment follow-up sequence supporting client retention
  • Owner no longer personally managing scheduling

Key Takeaway

No-show losses are a systems problem, not a client problem. Once a structured confirmation, reminder, and rescheduling workflow was in place — and a CRM captured client history that made follow-up possible — the business had the operational foundation to build real client retention.

DELIVERABLES: CRM Implementation ⋅ Scheduling SOP ⋅ Reminder Workflow ⋅ Client Onboarding Protocol ⋅ Retention Follow-Up Sequence

Result 03

Skilled Nursing Facility: Documentation Gaps to Compliance Confidence

A long-term care facility with Medicare documentation practices that were inconsistent, incomplete, and creating real compliance and reimbursement risk.

Before

  • Skilled charting inconsistent across nursing staff — no standard
  • Medicare medical necessity language absent from many nursing notes
  • No standardized documentation protocol for skilled nursing services
  • Therapy and nursing notes not aligned for PDPM compliance
  • Staff unaware of what documentation standards were required or why
  • Reactive approach to compliance — issues identified at survey

After

  • Skilled charting SOP with Medicare medical necessity language embedded
  • Documentation templates aligned to PDPM requirements
  • Staff education on compliance documentation standards and rationale
  • Interdisciplinary communication framework aligning therapy and nursing
  • Audit-ready documentation process established across nursing staff
  • Proactive compliance posture replacing reactive survey preparation

Key Takeaway

Documentation deficiencies in long-term care are not just compliance risks — they are revenue risks. When nursing notes do not demonstrate medical necessity with specificity, Medicare reimbursement is at risk. Building the right documentation framework protects both the facility and the patients it serves.

DELIVERABLES: Skilled Charting SOP ⋅ PDPM Documentation Templates ⋅ Staff Education Framework ⋅ IDT Communication Protocol ⋅ Audit Readiness System

Result 04

Healthcare Organization Launch: No Systems to Operational Launch Readiness

An organization preparing to launch with no documented processes, no SOPs, and no onboarding infrastructure — and a go-live date approaching.

Before

  • Zero written SOPs — every process lived in the founder's head
  • No staff onboarding framework or training documentation
  • No standardized client or patient intake process
  • No service agreement, intake form, or care coordination template
  • Founder was the single point of failure for every operational decision
  • Attempting to hire before systems existed to support new staff

After

  • Complete SOP library covering scheduling, documentation, communication, and escalation
  • Structured staff onboarding process with checklists and training milestones
  • Standardized intake documentation and client onboarding workflow
  • Service agreements, intake forms, and care coordination templates delivered
  • Founder able to delegate operational responsibilities at launch
  • Organization launched on time with every critical system documented and ready

Key Takeaway

Organizations that launch without operational systems compound their disorganization with every hire and every new client. Building the infrastructure before launch — not after — is what makes growth sustainable rather than chaotic.

DELIVERABLES: Complete SOP Library ⋅ Staff Onboarding Framework ⋅ Intake System ⋅ Service Agreements ⋅ Care Coordination Templates

The Pattern

What Every Engagement Has in Common

The Problem Is Always Systems

Across every engagement, the core problem is the same: critical processes exist in people's heads rather than in documented, repeatable systems. The clinical quality is often excellent. The operational infrastructure is not.

The Owner Is Always the Bottleneck

In every engagement, the founder or leader is making decisions and doing work that should be delegated — not because they want to, but because there are no documented systems for the team to follow independently.

The Fix Is Always Documentation

The solution is consistently the same: written SOPs, structured workflows, documented onboarding, and intake systems that produce the same result regardless of who is executing them.

The Result Is Always Freedom

When systems exist, leaders can lead. Providers can provide. Organizations can grow. Every engagement we deliver is ultimately about giving the leader their time, energy, and focus back — so they can do the work only they can do.
